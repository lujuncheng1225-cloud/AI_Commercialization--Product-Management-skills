# Terms Glossary Starter

> Status: starter draft. Keep what is useful, replace what is specific to your business.

## Product Terms

- Core workflow:
  - Meaning: the main repeatable task the product helps users complete
  - What it does not mean: any click, page view, or vanity interaction

- High-intent moment:
  - Meaning: the point where the user most clearly wants the job done now
  - What it does not mean: generic exposure to pricing or feature lists

## User Segment Terms

- High-value segment:
  - Meaning: users or accounts with repeat need, strong urgency, and higher willingness to pay
  - Boundary: not every active user belongs here

- Casual user:
  - Meaning: low-frequency user with weak urgency and low tolerance for friction
  - Boundary: may still be useful for acquisition, but should not define premium roadmap decisions

## Funnel Terms

- Activation:
  - Meaning: first meaningful completion of the core workflow
  - Calculation note: should be tied to value realization, not just setup completion

- Paid conversion:
  - Meaning: movement from free or trial state into actual commercial commitment
  - Calculation note: define the exact threshold per business model

- Retained active user:
  - Meaning: user who returns and completes the core workflow again within the relevant window
  - Calculation note: choose a window that matches real usage cadence

## AI Terms

- Task success rate:
  - Meaning: percentage of outputs that are actually usable for the intended task
  - Operational implication: better than generic “quality feels okay”

- Fallback:
  - Meaning: the safe degraded path when AI confidence or quality is not sufficient
  - Operational implication: required for many user-facing AI flows

- Unsupported claim:
  - Meaning: statement the system outputs without support from input or evidence
  - Operational implication: often a launch blocker in summarization, extraction, and recommendation flows

## Internal Shortcuts

- PRD:
  - Expanded form: product requirements document
  - How the agent should interpret it: structured decision and execution document, not a random feature wishlist

- Guardrail:
  - Expanded form: a metric or condition that protects against harmful side effects
  - How the agent should interpret it: must be explicit in launch and commercial decisions

## Ambiguous Terms To Avoid

- Better experience:
  - Why it is ambiguous: says nothing about who benefits or what improves
  - Preferred replacement: faster task completion, fewer errors, lower correction cost, clearer upgrade decision

- More engagement:
  - Why it is ambiguous: can mean shallow activity with no durable value
  - Preferred replacement: repeat usage of the core workflow, higher completion rate, stronger retained activity
